editTips and Tricks

Below are 10 tips and tricks on how to write heart-wrenching tragedy.

  1. Tragedy has general stages: anticipation, dream, frustration, nightmare, and then destruction.

  2. The tragic hero of the story should suffer some type of downfall by the end of the story.

  3. To pull at the reader’s heartstrings, the tragedy presented in the story should be one that anyone could face.

  4. Create characters that the reader can relate to and will care about.

  5. Ensure that by the end of the story, the tragedy has impacted the lives of the characters.

  6. Use metaphors and/or similes as they can evoke emotions which can then be overlaid on the scene – further meaning can be given to the scene than if the scene was simply described.

  7. Include unexpected twists to give hope – but still end with a downfall/tragedy.

  8. The plot should include a stage of building tension which is then eventually resolved – resolutions do not have to be positive.

  9. In addition to the standard tragedy there are others, such as the protagonist descending into evil or simply having the good guys lose to the villain.

  10. Tragic victory is a very powerful form of tragedy as conflicting emotions result from a situation that would otherwise be joyous.

For more information, visit the following websites from which the above points were gathered: 

http://thewritepractice.com/basic-tragedy/

https://www.wikihow.com/Write-a-Tragedy

http://www.danielarenson.com/tipWritingTragedies.aspx

editHow to Write Tragedy

ISBN: 978-1475004953

Publication Date: May 6, 2012

The Emotion Thesaurus’ purpose is to provide a guide for writers to write convincing character emotions.

This book highlights 75 emotions and their accompanying body language, identifies common problems when writing emotions, and explains how to show a character’s emotions rather than just telling the reader the character’s emotions.

Book on Amazon

ISBN: 978-1534846807

Publication Date: September 10, 2016

Emotional Beats’ purpose is to provide writers with a resource on how to effectively convey character emotions.

This book contains a thesaurus of numerous emotions that writers can use in their stories and includes information on how to show emotions instead of telling them and how to represent emotions through actions.  

Book on Amazon

ISBN: 978-1492213390

Publication Date: August 20, 2013

Writing with a Heavy Heart’s purpose is to help writers create deeply emotional stories that cause readers to empathize.

This book includes how to write events that pull at the heartstrings, how to use personal experiences to create realistic emotions in characters, and how to use subtle hints to portray what a character is feeling.

Book on Amazon

ISBN: 978-1542578028

Publication Date: February 5, 2017

Writing Vivid Emotions’ purpose is to provide an all-in-one resource for writers on how to write powerful emotions.

This book contains information on how writers can use vocabulary to convey various emotions without blatantly stating the emotion, how to layer various emotions with varying intensity, how to express emotions through body language, and mistakes to avoid when writing character emotions.
